-- Here’s how you play our game --
  • 1. In Project Mindsweeper you have a name. So enter that and decide a password, and you can begin!
  • 2. The objective of the game is simple, to get to the next level.
  • 3. You’ll need to download the Zwibe app to pass a total of 4 levels in the game. So make sure you download it here. They’re fairly simple as well.
  • 4. There are multiple ways of going to the next level:
    • a. Clicking on sections of an image
    • b. Typing an answer into the answer box.
    • c. Changing the URL.
  • 5. The answers are not case­-sensitive, which means that if you type “Machi Rediyaa?” or “machi rediyaa?”it wouldn’t make a difference.
  • 6. We try to cover most possible answers for a level, but keep trying for the right combination.
  • 7. Now, the clues, oh my God, look at the joy on your face, ‘we get effing clues!’. The clues can be found:
    • a. In the page source (right click and click on ‘View Page Source’).
    • b. The title of the level page.
    • c. The URL.
  • 8. There are some levels which require you to manipulate the URL of the page for either a hint or the answer itself, you will know when you have to do so. Either change the last element, or add an extension.
  • 9. Adding an extension to the URL will present you with a downloadable file, like .mp3, .png, .jpg, or .txt.
  • 10. There are also some levels which lead you down a wrong path, so tread lightly. (Heisenberg eshtyle)
  • 11. Most importantly, on the Facebook event page there will be discussion forums for every five levels. Make sure you only discuss the finding of the answer there without revealing major hints or the answer itself.
  • 12. Any revelation of the answer on these forums will lead to disqualification.
  • 13. And you are welcome to discuss solving the treasure hunt with your friends in exchange for recreational substances (also the organisers). But don't provide others with direct answers, it is a competition after all and you can use Google. Doing that would be tantamount to saying they're better than Google.
  • 14. We have no restrictions on the number of attempts, so do abuse this rule outrightly. We must mention that there is one keyword that will get you through every level. We call this the Skeleton Key.
  • 15. As the difficulty levels increase, so does our reluctance to give out obvious clues. In case we find many players to be stuck at certain levels, a blatant push will be provided from our end. But the later levels of the game are made in such a way that players will have to spend some time wrapping their head around the clues, rather than expect the same to be solved in a matter of minutes.
  • 16. Do try your best, and be a little patient as far as hints are concerned.
